# Scout — 5 Ad Concepts

---

## Ad Concept #1: "The Locker Room Network"

**Headline:**
"Your teammates from 1985 are your network now."

**Body Copy:**
"Every Cornell athlete who's made it on Wall Street, in Silicon Valley, or at McKinsey? They wore the same jersey you do.

Scout connects you with 18,000+ Cornell athlete alumni — by sport, by industry, by company. No cold emails. No awkward LinkedIn DMs. Just warm introductions through the one thing you share: being Big Red.

AI-crafted outreach messages. Pipeline tracking. Swipe-to-discover. Built for athletes, by athletes.

You didn't build your career alone on the field. You don't have to build it alone off the field either.

**Join Scout. It's free. It's yours. It's already waiting."**

**Visual Description:**
Split-image composition. Left side: a black-and-white photo of a Cornell hockey or football team huddled up from the 1980s (vintage, archival feel, sepia-toned). Right side: a clean iPhone mockup of the Scout Discover screen showing an alumni card — same sport, same school, modern day. The connection arrow visually bridges the two eras. Overlay text in Cornell red (#B31B1B): "55 Years. 40 Sports. 18,000+ Alumni." Bottom third has the Scout logo and "Download on the App Store / Google Play" badges.

**Platform:** Instagram (feed post + Story), Facebook

**Target Audience:** Current student-athletes (sophomores–seniors actively recruiting for internships/jobs)

**Why It Would Work:**
This ad taps into the deepest emotional current in college athletics: the bond of wearing the same jersey. Every athlete has heard some version of "once a teammate, always a teammate." By pairing a vintage team photo with a modern app interface, we make the abstract concept of "alumni network" visceral and immediate. The copy avoids corporate MBA-speak and talks like an actual athlete talks. The "warm introductions" framing removes the fear of cold outreach, which is the #1 barrier students face. This ad works because it doesn't sell a product — it validates an identity.

## Ad Concept #2: "5 Minutes, One Message"

**Headline:**
"I messaged 3 alumni. Got 2 replies. Landed an interview."

**Body Copy:**
Marcus ran track at Cornell. He'd heard about Scout from a teammate, opened it during a study break, and swiped through alumni from the cross country and track program.

He found a VP at a bulge bracket bank. And a senior engineer at a FAANG company. And a consultant at MBB.

Scout's AI wrote the first message for him — referencing their shared sport, their overlapping years, the connection only Cornell athletes understand.

Both replied within 48 hours.

Marcus has two coffee chats scheduled for next week.

**One app. One shared identity. Endless possibilities.**

Join the hundreds of Cornell athletes already building their careers on Scout.

**Visual Description:**
Three-panel horizontal Instagram carousel.
- Panel 1 (left): A student-athlete's perspective — phone screen showing Scout's "Generate Message" modal. Blue chatbot-style "AI is writing your message..." indicator. Copy overlay: "AI writes the first message. You make the connection."
- Panel 2 (center): Split inbox — two reply notifications from alumni in different industries. Copy overlay: "2 replies. Different industries. One app."
- Panel 3 (right): A calendar screenshot showing "Coffee Chat: Goldman Sachs" + "Coffee Chat: Google." Copy overlay: "From swipe to sit-down. 48 hours."
Throughout: warm off-white backgrounds with Cornell red accents, Scout logo in bottom right.

**Platform:** Instagram Carousel, TikTok slideshow

**Target Audience:** Current student-athletes who know they should network but find it awkward or time-consuming

**Why It Would Work:**
Social proof is the strongest conversion driver for college students. Marcus is relatable — he's not a valedictorian or a startup founder, he's a regular athlete who used a tool. The specific numbers ("3 alumni, 2 replies, landed an interview") are concrete and believable. The AI-generated message angle removes the biggest friction point: "I don't know what to say." Students who've wanted to reach out but felt awkward will see this and think, "If Marcus can do it, so can I." The 48-hour turnaround creates urgency and implies the alumni are actually responsive.

## Ad Concept #3: "The Unfair Advantage"

**Headline:**
"Other schools have alumni networks. You have a brotherhood."

**Body Copy:**
"Your resume says 'Cornell.' Your network says 'Cornell athlete.'

That's the difference between a cold application and a warm intro. Between 'I'll keep your resume on file' and 'Let me grab coffee with you.'

Scout is the only platform built specifically for Cornell athlete alumni. 18,000+ profiles. 40+ sports. 55 years of Big Red.

Here's what makes it different:
- **By sport:** Find alumni who ran the same plays you did
- **By industry:** Filter by finance, tech, consulting — wherever you want to go
- **With AI:** Write better messages in 10 seconds than you could in 10 minutes
- **With context:** Every profile shows their sport, their year, their path from Schoellkopf to the C-suite

**You've already got the alumni edge. Scout just puts it in your pocket.**

**Visual Description:**
Hero image: A student-athlete in full Cornell gear (pinnie, shorts, sneakers) sitting on a bench in Schoellkopf Field or Barton Hall, looking down at their phone with the Scout app open. The lighting is golden hour — warm, aspirational. Overlay text, large and bold: "YOUR NETWORK IS BIGGER THAN YOU THINK." Below, a horizontal scroll of 4–5 alumni profile cards (headshot + name + company) — diverse industries, diverse sports. The word "Scout" in the Cornell red wordmark at the bottom. Clean, premium, athletic.

**Platform:** Instagram feed post, Facebook, LinkedIn (slightly adapted copy)

**Target Audience:** Current student-athletes + incoming freshmen

**Why It Would Work:**
The headline draws a clear contrast between Scout and generic alumni networks (LinkedIn, Cornell's official alumni directory). "Other schools have alumni networks. You have a brotherhood" is a line that resonates with every athlete who's ever been in a huddle or a team bus. It positions Scout not as a utility app but as an extension of the team experience. The visual of an athlete in uniform on their phone creates a natural, authentic scene — this is what a Cornell athlete actually looks like during downtime. The specific location reference (Schoellkopf) grounds the ad in Cornell's physical campus, making it feel real and specific rather than generic and templated.

## Ad Concept #4: "Two Minutes"

**Headline:**
"Two minutes. No meetings. Just be findable."

**Body Copy:**
"You know what it's like to get that DM from a kid on your old team: 'Hey, I'm applying to [Company] — any advice?'

You want to help. You really do. But you're busy. And formal mentorship feels like a commitment.

Scout is the easy way to give back.

- **Claim your profile** in under 2 minutes
- **Students find you** by your sport, industry, or company
- **You reply when you can** — no calendar, no meetings, no pressure
- **Flag outdated info** — our admin team keeps your profile accurate

That's it. No monthly commitment. No mentorship contract. Just be in the directory and respond when a kid from your old team reaches out.

**Remember who helped you get your first job. Be that person now.**

22,000+ Cornell athletes are waiting to hear from you.

**Visual Description:**
Clean, professional LinkedIn-optimized image. Left side: text-heavy with the headline and key bullet points in a serif font (elegant, alumni-appropriate). Right side: a warm candid photo of an alum at their desk — mid-30s, friendly smile, a Cornell hat or mug visible in the background. Subtle watermark: the Scout "S" logo. Color palette: navy blue background (not Cornell red — this is LinkedIn, need to blend), white text, a single Cornell red accent line separating sections. Bottom: "scout.app/alumni" in discreet text.

**Platform:** LinkedIn (primary), email newsletter (adapted)

**Target Audience:** Alumni aged 28–55, employed in professional services (finance, tech, consulting, law, healthcare)

**Why It Would Work:**
Alumni are the hardest audience to convert because they're busy and skeptical of time commitments. This ad directly addresses that skepticism head-on: "Two minutes. No meetings." The headline is a promise of low friction. The body validates the alumni's good intentions ("You want to help. You really do.") while removing the guilt of not doing enough. The phrase "be findable" is a much lower bar than "be a mentor," making the action feel easy. The emotional closer ("Remember who helped you get your first job.") is a classic but effective appeal to alumni nostalgia and gratitude. The 22,000 number creates FOMO — "other alumni are already doing this."

## Ad Concept #5: "The Circles Effect"

**Headline:**
"It's not what you know. It's who your teammates know."

**Body Copy:**
"Everyone says 'network, network, network.' But the best network is the one you already have.

Scout's **Circles** feature shows you the alumni your teammates already know. Not random cold contacts — warm paths through your own team.

Here's how it works:
1. Your teammate saved an alum from your sport
2. Circles shows that connection in a visual map
3. You ask your teammate: 'Hey, can you intro me?'
4. Warm intro > cold email. Every time.

You're one conversation away from breaking into your dream industry.

And that conversation is probably through someone you already share a locker room with.

**Scout. Built for Cornell Athletics. Free for every athlete.**

**Visual Description:**
Instagram carousel (3 slides).
- Slide 1: Full-screen screenshot of the Circles network visualization — interlocking nodes (alumni) connected by lines, colored by industry (finance = navy, tech = blue, consulting = green). The student's profile is a Cornell red node in the center. Copy overlay: "Your network. Visualized."
- Slide 2: Zoom in on one cluster — "Your teammate Jack knows Sarah Chen (Goldman Sachs VP, Women's Soccer). You're one intro away." Arrow drawn from your profile → Jack's profile → Sarah's profile. Copy: "Warm paths. Not cold outreach."
- Slide 3: Simple CTA screen. "Open Scout. See your Circles. Make the ask." Big button: "Discover Your Network."

**Platform:** Instagram carousel, TikTok explainer video, in-app feature highlight

**Target Audience:** Current student-athletes (especially juniors/seniors in recruiting season)

**Why It Would Work:**
Circles is Scout's most unique feature — the thing that differentiates it from LinkedIn or any generic alumni directory. This ad makes the value proposition visual and simple: warm intros beat cold outreach. The "ask your teammate" framing is socially normal — athletes are comfortable asking teammates for help. The ad reframes networking from a lonely, grinding activity into a collaborative one: you and your teammates, working your collective network. The visual map design is inherently shareable and Instagram-friendly. This ad works because it makes the latent value of the team's accumulated network explicit and actionable.
