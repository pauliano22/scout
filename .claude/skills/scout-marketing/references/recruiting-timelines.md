# Recruiting Timelines — the "three clocks" positioning

The single strongest Education vein for Scout. Core reframe (use this, not a finance-only story):

> **There is no single "college recruiting season." Every industry runs on a different clock. Scout helps students know what season they're actually in, what to do next, and who to talk to before the window closes.**

Positioning line to lean on: **"Scout helps you know what season you're in."** Simple, emotional, true, and it speaks to *every* athlete — not just finance.

## The three clocks (the centerpiece graphic)
| Clock | Industries | Student problem | Scout's job |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Early Pipeline** | Finance, consulting, big tech, quant, major banks | "I didn't know I had to start this early." | Tell them what to do *before applications open*. |
| **Structured Fall Cycle** | Accounting, corporate finance, engineering, rotational programs, healthcare/pharma | "So many programs and deadlines." | Organize targets, deadlines, alumni, prep. |
| **Just-in-Time / Relationship-Driven** | Real estate, startups, nonprofits, policy, media, hospitality | "There aren't clear postings yet — what do I do?" | Turn networking into a step-by-step path. |

Supporting facts (cite loosely, verify before hard claims): NACE 2025 — ~70% of employers recruit in both fall and spring; offer timing has shifted, 1/3+ of offers now land in spring/summer. Intern→full-time conversion hit **63.1%** (2024–25), a 5-year high (PwC reports 90%+ for its interns). Internships are the main path to full-time in most tracks — a recurring post angle.

## Per-industry timing (by school year / month — VERIFY each cycle, dates drift)
- **Finance / banking / private markets** — networking starts sophomore fall–spring for junior-summer internships; internships recruit ~Jan–June (competitive) and June–fall (major banks); full-time June–Sept, often via intern conversion. *Finance searches sophomores for junior-summer roles; shifting earlier, ~1 yr ahead.*
- **Consulting** — network 1–2 semesters ahead, case prep 3–6 months ahead; apps often open late June/July, interviews late summer/fall; roles June–Nov. Top firms have hard summer deadlines.
- **Tech / product / data** — big firms recruit Sept–Oct (fill fast, apply within weeks of posting); many others Jan–April; startups roll through spring.
- **Accounting / professional services** — sophomore year for accounting-track; winter internships ~1 yr before graduation; summer internships June–Aug; strong intern→FT pipeline.
- **Corporate / rotational / leadership dev** — apply late summer/fall (Aug–Nov), some structured programs recruit a year early. "Hidden gems" angle.
- **Commercial real estate** — highly networking-driven; build relationships in the fall, most hiring spring (Jan–May); some institutional platforms post in fall a year ahead.
- **Engineering / manufacturing / supply chain** — early fall through winter (Oct–Feb), career fairs matter; co-ops/internships → full-time.
- **Healthcare / pharma / biotech** — corporate roles fall–winter (Sept–March); smaller labs/research later (Jan–April).
- **Government / policy / IR** — federal + clearance roles apply early (some 12–18 months ahead for clearances); think tanks/public affairs hire later; Pathways internships → federal FT.
- **Marketing / comms / media / entertainment** — year-round, peak Sept–March; often one-off roles, not big classes; portfolio + referrals matter.
- **Nonprofit / social impact** — mostly as-needed, closer to graduation (Nov–April); relationship-driven.
- **Startups / entrepreneurship** — just-in-time, close to graduation; roles often *created through outreach*, not postings. "Scout helps you create the conversation that leads to the role."

## The Scout roadmap (7 stages — great carousel spine, and the product story)
1. **Discover** — "What paths even exist for someone like me?"
2. **Know the clock** — "Am I early, on time, or already late?" (the big value)
3. **Build the plan** — "What should I do this week?" (resume, target list, 3 alumni, 2 cases…)
4. **Find the right alumni** — "Who has done what I'm trying to do?" (industry/role/sport/company/location) — strongest for athletes; the alumni tie is natural and trusted.
5. **Prepare outreach** — "What do I say without sounding awkward?" (first message, follow-up, coffee-chat Qs, thank-you, referral ask)
6. **Apply & interview** — "What do I need to be ready for?" (case/technical/coding/market/writing prep per track)
7. **Convert or pivot** — "Double down, widen, or switch?" (return offer, or pivot banking→CRE, consulting→corp strategy, policy→public affairs…)
